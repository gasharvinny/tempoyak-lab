Scientific Foundation & Kinetic Modeling

The Mathematics of Fermentation Kinetics

In Agro-Product Engineering, lactic acid fermentation is modeled as a sigmoidal growth function. The accumulation of lactic acid, \(f(x)\), in grams per liter (g/L) over fermentation time \(x\) in hours is described by the modified Logistic Model:

Primary Function (Lactic Acid Yield)
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}} + C$$

To discover the instantaneous rate of acid production at any exact time \(x\), we apply the quotient and chain rules to take the first derivative, \(f'(x)\):

First Derivative (Production Rate)
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$
Parameter Constants used in this model (Based on actual MARDI kinetics):
  • \(L = 14.5 \text{ g/L}\) (Maximum capacity/saturation limit of lactic acid yield in durian pulp)
  • \(k = 0.16 \text{ hr}^{-1}\) (The bacterial growth rate constant under optimal ambient Malaysian temperatures)
  • \(x_0 = 20.0 \text{ hours}\) (Inflection point: point of maximum growth rate)
  • \(C = 0.50 \text{ g/L}\) (Initial lactic acid concentration immediately post-inoculation)

The Bioscience Scenario: Tempoyak

Tempoyak is an authentic Malaysian fermented condiment made from the pulp of durian (Durio zibethinus). During natural or controlled inoculations, Lactic Acid Bacteria (LAB) such as Lactobacillus plantarum, Lactobacillus brevis, and Leuconostoc mesenteroides utilize soluble sugars (sucrose, glucose, fructose) in the creamy pulp, converting them primarily to lactic acid, secondary organic acids, and volatile compounds.

The derivative \(f'(x)\) is critical because:

  • pH Stabilization: Rapid drop in pH prevents the growth of hazardous pathogens (like Escherichia coli and Salmonella) and spoilage molds. The faster \(f'(x)\) peaks, the safer the fermentation curve.
  • Heat & Gas Management: Fermentation is exothermic. Peak metabolic activity \(f'(x)\) matches peak heat output. Food technologists use this peak to configure bioreactor cooling jackets in industrial processing plants.
  • Texture & Flavor Synthesis: Lactic acid breaks down celluloses and starches, softening the durian pulp to achieve the characteristic smooth, pasty mouthfeel of tempoyak.
